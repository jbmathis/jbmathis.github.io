General Motors Internship
Date: May - July 2017
Work Environment: Individual with Mentor
Role: Quality Data Intern

Summary

For three months, I interned at General Motors in their Quality Data and Strategy Development department. My main project entailed revamping a market research tool they frequently used. The tool ingested data from JD Power's annual APEAL survey so Project Managers could analyze scores and benchmark against competitors. Completing this project required exploring the current tool in MS Excel, designing a new version based on user feedback, and developing it in MS Access. This involved designing a relational database and writing complex SQL queries to power the interface. Unfortunately, I am not allowed to share photos or specifics about the interface because it handles confidential data.

Background Information

At the time of my internship, GM was going through a business deal to sell Opel, one of its subsidiary car companies. The market research tool was created by a third party and provided to Opel, which unfortunately meant GM would lose access to it once Opel was sold. I was tasked with developing a new version so GM could have an improved, in-house tool to analyze customer satisfaction data.

Project Process

I worked independently on this project, but regularly met with a mentor to show progress and discuss roadblocks. Below you can see the process I went through to complete this project, ending with a final demonstration of my tool to GM leadership.

Challenges and Successes

Since I was the first and only intern in the department at the time, my role was not completely defined. Though this uncharted territory was daunting at first, I was able to pick a project tailored to my interests and also trailblaze a path for future interns. The independent nature of the work challenged me to identify and solve many issues on my own, which was especially difficult since I was working with unfamiliar tools and learning a new language (VBA). This forced me to learn at a rapid pace, but resulted in a product I could proudly call my own.

Final Thoughts

This was my first time experiencing the stresses and opportunities of working at a large company. I struggled and made mistakes, but the challenges helped me to grow both intellectually and professionally. Seeing how happy my redesigned tool made GM employees was the most rewarding part of the experience.
